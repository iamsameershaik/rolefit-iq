AI Automation Engineer
Company: FlowOps Labs
Location: Remote UK
Salary: £45,000 – £65,000

ABOUT THE ROLE
FlowOps Labs helps operations teams automate repetitive work using AI agents, workflow automation, and internal tools. We are looking for an AI Automation Engineer who can rapidly prototype, validate, and deploy AI-powered workflow applications.

RESPONSIBILITIES
- Build AI automation workflows that combine LLM APIs, business rules, API integrations, and human approval steps.
- Create full-stack prototypes using React, TypeScript, Python, and serverless functions.
- Integrate OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, or AWS Bedrock into internal business workflows.
- Build dashboards for workflow status, review queues, operational metrics, and exception handling.
- Work with stakeholders to identify automation opportunities, define acceptance criteria, and measure business impact.
- Implement prompt templates, structured outputs, retry handling, guardrails, and logging.
- Connect systems such as CRMs, spreadsheets, document stores, ticketing tools, and internal databases.
- Document workflows, architecture decisions, and operational handover notes.

REQUIRED SKILLS
- Practical experience building LLM-powered applications or automations.
- TypeScript, React, and Python experience.
- API integration experience.
- Understanding of prompt engineering, structured JSON outputs, and guardrails.
- Experience with SQL databases and data transformation.
- Ability to work with non-technical stakeholders and translate requirements into working systems.
- Strong ownership and rapid prototyping ability.

NICE TO HAVE
- Experience with Make, Zapier, n8n, Vapi, Voiceflow, or similar automation platforms.
- Supabase, PostgreSQL, Netlify, or serverless deployment experience.
- Familiarity with AWS services or AWS Bedrock.
- Experience with document processing, retrieval, or internal knowledge assistants.
- Experience designing human-in-the-loop review workflows.

INTERVIEW FOCUS
We will ask candidates to explain how they choose what to automate, how they prevent AI mistakes from reaching production, and how they measure whether an AI workflow is actually useful.
