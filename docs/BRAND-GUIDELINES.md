# RoleFit IQ Brand Identity Guidelines

## Creative North Star

**If Sony designed an AI career intelligence product in 2026.**

RoleFit IQ should feel like a precision software instrument: serious, academic, engineered, and premium through restraint.

## Brand Essence

**Evidence-first career intelligence.**

RoleFit IQ is an evidence engine for comparing a CV against multiple job descriptions. It should feel less like a chatbot and more like a senior engineer reviewing an application systematically.

## User Promise

**Understand your fit before you apply.**

## Logo Concept: The Missing Fit

The brand mark is an intentionally incomplete 3x3 grid. The bottom-right unit is absent.

This missing square represents:

- the role the user is trying to fit into;
- the gap between a CV and a job description;
- the final evidence point needed before a decision;
- RoleFit IQ's role in helping the user locate the missing fit.

Do not turn the mark into a robot, brain, sparkle, or chat bubble. The absence is the idea.

## Typography

Recommended implementation stack:

- Sans: `Inter` or `Geist`
- Mono: `Geist Mono`, `IBM Plex Mono`, or `JetBrains Mono`

Usage:

- Large sans-serif headings for confidence and hierarchy.
- Monospace only for technical labels, citations, evidence IDs, scores, file names, and metadata.
- Avoid decorative display fonts.

## Visual Principles

1. **Evidence over decoration** — every visual element should imply structure, comparison, evidence, or decision support.
2. **Precision through restraint** — fewer elements, stronger hierarchy, cleaner spacing.
3. **Visible engineering** — grids, index labels, citations, technical metadata, reference lines.
4. **Human decision support** — the product supports judgment; it does not pretend to replace it.
5. **Modern retro, not nostalgia** — abstract Sony-era discipline without parody.

## Things We Never Do

Avoid:

- robot heads;
- brain icons;
- generic AI gradients;
- neon cyberpunk;
- magic-wand metaphors;
- SaaS cartoon illustrations;
- glassmorphism overload;
- AI sparkles;
- crypto-style marks;
- ATS fearmongering;
- exaggerated hiring promises.

## Core Copy

Use:

- Evidence-first career intelligence.
- Understand your fit before you apply.
- Compare one CV against multiple job descriptions.
- Every result is supported by retrieved evidence.
- Decision support for serious applications.

Avoid:

- Unlock your dream job.
- Supercharge your CV.
- Beat the ATS.
- Get hired instantly.
- AI magic for resumes.
